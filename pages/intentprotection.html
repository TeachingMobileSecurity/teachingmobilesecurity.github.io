Intent Protection

Introduction

In Android, Intents are messaging objects used to request something from another app component. App components can be thought of as the basic parts of an Android app. An example of an app component is an Activity.

Intents can also be used to deliver needed information to the app component being asked to do something.

The problem is that sending data between apps can be insecure in Android. For example, Android will automatically export an Activity for other apps to use if certain requirements are met. See: the Android Developer Guide for more information on this.1
